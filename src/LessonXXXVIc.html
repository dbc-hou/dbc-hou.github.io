LATIN FOR THE END OF THE WORLD

Vocabulary and Syntax—Verbs

The volume presented only six new verbs, all related to new nouns, to add to the 100-plus already introduced. For this exercise, translate each of the phrases below into the correcct Latin forms of the verbs shown in bold.

clārō, clārāre

  1. they clarify
    clārant
  2. they used to clarify
    clārābant
  3. they used to be clarified
    clārābantur

gregō, gregāre

  1. you (s.) are herded
    clārant
  2. you (s.) will be herded
    clārābant
  3. you (pl.) will be herded
    clārābantur

luecō, lucēre

  1. I shine
    luceō
  2. we shine
    lucēmus
  3. they used to be clarified
    lucēbāmus

obscūrō, obscūrāre

  1. it was darkening
    obscūrābat
  2. it will darken
    obscūrabit
  3. they will darken
    obscūrābunt

offuscō, offuscāre

  1. they will be darkened
    offuscābuntur
  2. y'all will be darkened
    offuscābiminī
  3. be darkened!
    offuscāminī

ōrdinō, ōrdināre

  1. we are putting in order
    ōrdināmus
  2. we are put in order
    ōrdināmur
  3. we were being put in order
    ōrdinābāmur
