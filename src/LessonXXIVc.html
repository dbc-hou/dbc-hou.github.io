Vocabulary—Adjectives

Give the basic meanings of each. Then give the correct form(s) to agree with the noun to the right of each adjective. (We've made Thrāx especially easy.)

Those marked with (2) have at least two correct forms to agree with them. Some forms can represent multiple combinations of case and number, and yet just one adjective form may agree with them all—e.g., #12. Most of the adjective forms will have the same endings as the nouns—but not all of them!

One last note: three of the nouns are uter gender in this text, but masculine in standard Latin dictionaries. In the case of any gender ambiguity, see if you can supply both the masculine and feminine forms.
