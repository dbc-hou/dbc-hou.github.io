LATIN FOR THE END OF THE WORLD

Verbs, Nouns, and Adjectives

Unlike the previous Review chapters, the vocabulary portion of this one will present sets of related words together.

  • Some of the related words are from previous volumes, presented here (in italics) just to remind you of their relation to recent vocabulary.
  • Some are words that we have not introduced formally, but you should be able to deduce their English meanings.

For each verb, see if you can remember the principal parts as well as the meanings. To check your answers, see this page.

For each noun, see if you can remember the Genitive form and gender. (Some of them are plural only.) Check your answers here.

For each adjective, see if you can remember how to form its comparative and superlative degrees (where possible). Again, we have a separate page to view that information.

NOTE: Because Volume IV introduces a variety of suffixes, and thus added so many words, this would be a good time to review the vocabulary lists in Reviews I through III.
