LATIN FOR THE END OF THE WORLD

Adjective and Adverb Mutations

The adjectives below are presented in their dictionary forms: Nominative singular masculine, positive degree. Make the requested changes in case, number, gender, degree, or part of speech. The first set of mutations is done for you. (NOTES: (2) means that there are two possible answers. Some of the changes requested will require no actual change in form!)

  1. mollis
    1. Change to neuter: molle
    2. Change that to plural: mollia
    3. Change that to comparative: molliora
  2. grāmineus
    1. Change to superlative:
      māximē grāmineus
    2. Change that to Ablative:
      māximē grāmineō
    3. Change that to feminine:
      māximē grāmineā
  3. salūber
    1. Change to feminine:
      salūbris
    2. Change that to comparative:
      salūbrior
    3. Change that to Dative:
      salūbriorī
  4. sānus
    1. Change to Accusative:
      sānum
    2. Change that to plural:
      sānōs
    3. Change that to superlative:
      sānissimōs
  5. clēmēns
    1. Change to neuter:
      clēmēns
    2. Change that to comparative:
      clēmentius
    3. Change that to Genitive:
      clēmentiōris
  6. mendāx
    1. Change to plural (2):
      mendācēs, mendācia
    2. Change that to superlative (2):
      mendācissimī/-a
    3. Change that to Accusative (2):
      mendācissimōs/-a
  7. rūsticus
    1. Change to Vocative:
      rūstice
    2. Change that to plural:
      rūsticī
    3. Change that to comparative:
      rūsticiōrēs
  8. decorus
    1. Change to an adverb:
      decorē
    2. Change that to comparative:
      decorius
    3. Change that to superlative:
      decorissimē
  9. populāris
    1. Change to superlative (2):
      populārissimus/-a
    2. Change that to an adverb:
      populārissimē
    3. Change that to comparative:
      populārius
  10. vernālis
    1. Change to an adverb:
      vernāliter
    2. Change that back to an adjective, but superlative (2):
      vernālissimus/-a
    3. Change that to neuter:
      vernālissimum
